About

Curious about images, geometry, and how machines learn from both.

I am a Ph.D. student at the Biomedical Image Intelligence Lab, Nara Institute of Science and Technology (NAIST), where I work on medical image analysis and privacy-preserving learning. My current research focuses on federated learning for quantitative musculoskeletal CT analysis, with an emphasis on segmentation, cross-institutional generalization, and clinically meaningful imaging biomarkers.

I am interested in how medical imaging methods can remain reliable across different institutions, scanners, protocols, and patient populations. Beyond my central medical-imaging work, I am also curious about image denoising, computer graphics, ray tracing, geometric representations, and the path from segmented voxels to meaningful 3D anatomical structures.

My academic path began with bachelor’s studies in Mathematics at the University of Waterloo. I later joined the Faculty of Medicine at Osaka University as a research student, completed my master’s degree at NAIST, spent time at Karlsruhe Institute of Technology in Germany as an exchange student, and now continue my research at NAIST as a Ph.D. student.

Outside the lab, I enjoy photography, bouldering, tennis, travel, and exploring local food. I like collecting small observations from unfamiliar places and learning topics that sit slightly beyond my current research—especially when they connect visual understanding, geometry, and real-world systems.

Research

Research interests

Quantitative medical imaging, federated learning, and geometric computational methods for robust and privacy-aware healthcare AI.

quantitative anatomy

Musculoskeletal Analysis

I develop automated pipelines for segmentation, tracking, and quantitative analysis of muscle, adipose tissue, bone, and related anatomical structures in 3D/4D clinical imaging, including CT and X-ray. The goal is to support longitudinal assessment of musculoskeletal health and body composition.

privacy-aware AI

Federated Learning

I investigate machine learning frameworks for distributed clinical datasets, with a focus on domain shift, site heterogeneity, robust evaluation, and multi-institutional deployment.

graphics meets imaging

Geometry, Rendering & Denoising

I am interested in bridging computer graphics and medical imaging through geometric representations, surface reconstruction, image denoising, and rendering-inspired methods for medical image analysis.
